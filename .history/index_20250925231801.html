📚 Master Reading List for Distressed Investing

1. Foundational Books

  • Stephen G. Moyer – Distressed Debt Analysis: Strategies for Speculative Investors (2005)
  • Martin Whitman & Fernando Diz – Distressed Investing: Principles and Technique
  • Joseph Sarachek – The Distressed Investing Playbook (NYU Stern)
  • Frank Gatto – The Credit Investor’s Handbook
  • Hilary Rosenberg – The Vulture Investors
  • Howard Marks – The Most Important Thing
  • Harlan D. Platt – The Art of Distressed M&A
  • Aswath Damodaran – The Dark Side of Valuation

2. Core Textbook / Chapter Readings

  • Berk & DeMarzo – Corporate Finance (5th ed.), Chapter 16 “Financial Distress”
  • Altman & Hotchkiss – Corporate Financial Distress and Bankruptcy (3rd ed., 2006), Ch. 5, 8, 11
  • Kaiser & Young – The Blue Line Imperative (2013), Ch. 3, 4, 11
  • McKinsey & Co. – Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies (7th ed.), Ch. 11–14

3. Academic & Practitioner Articles

  • A Primer on Distressed Investing – Moyer, Martin & Martin (JACF, 2012)
  • Hedge Funds and Chapter 11 – Jiang, Li & Wang (Journal of Finance, 2012)
  • Rent Extraction by Super-Priority Lenders – Eckbo, Li & Wang (SSRN/Tuck, 2019–2020)
  • The Hertz Maneuver – Casey & Macey (U. Chicago L. Rev. Online, 2020)
  • The Expected Costs of Distress – Glover (JFE, 2016)
  • The Ownership and Trading of Debt Claims in Chapter 11 – Ivashina, Iverson & Smith (JFE, 2016)
  • The Myth of Creditor Sabotage – Buccola, Mah & Zhang (U. Chicago L. Rev., 2020)
  • A Market-Based Study of the Cost of Default – Davydenko, Strebulaev & Zhao (RFS, 2012)
  • How Costly is Financial (Not Economic) Distress? – Andrade & Kaplan (Journal of Finance, 1998)
  • Coming Through in a Crisis – Stuart Gilson (JACF, 2012)
  • Managing for Value 2.0 – Kaiser & Young (JACF, 2014)
  • The Hazards of Growth – Kaiser & Young (JACF, 2015)
  • Strategies for Business Turnaround and Recovery – Schoenberg, Collier & Bowman (European Business Review, 2013)
  • Antibankruptcy – Baird & Rasmussen (Yale Law Journal, 2009–10)
  • Chapter 11: Not Perfect, But Better than the Alternatives – Sprayregen et al. (J. Bankr. L. & Prac., 2005)
  • The Race to the Starting Line – Sprayregen et al. (2010)
  • Need for Speed – Brian K. Tester et al., ABI
  • Bankruptcy Court is Latest Battleground for Traders – Spector & McGinty, WSJ (2010)
  • Rights Offerings in Bankruptcy: More than New Capital – J. of Insolvency & Restructuring Advisors (2011)
  • Trends in Distressed Debt Investing – Maryland Law Review

4. Legal & Judicial Resources

  • U.S. Courts – Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Basics (online)
  • Hon. Christopher S. Sontchi – Valuation Methodologies: A Judge’s View (Am. Bankr. Inst. L. Rev., 2012)
  • James H.M. Sprayregen – Statements to ABI Commission on Chapter 11 Reform
  • Sample Plan of Reorganization & credit agreements (course materials)
