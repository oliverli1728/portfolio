📚 Master Reading List for Distressed Investing

1. Foundational Books

  • Stephen G. Moyer – Distressed Debt Analysis: Strategies for Speculative Investors (2005)
  • Martin Whitman & Fernando Diz – Distressed Investing: Principles and Technique
  • Joseph Sarachek – The Distressed Investing Playbook (NYU Stern)
  • Frank Gatto – The Credit Investor’s Handbook
  • Hilary Rosenberg – The Vulture Investors
  • Howard Marks – The Most Important Thing
  • Harlan D. Platt – The Art of Distressed M&A
  • Aswath Damodaran – The Dark Side of Valuation

2. Core Textbook / Chapter Readings

  • Berk & DeMarzo – Corporate Finance (5th ed.), Chapter 16 “Financial Distress”
  • Altman & Hotchkiss – Corporate Financial Distress and Bankruptcy (3rd ed., 2006), Ch. 5, 8, 11
  • Kaiser & Young – The Blue Line Imperative (2013), Ch. 3, 4, 11
  • McKinsey & Co. – Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies (7th ed.), Ch. 11–14

3. Academic & Practitioner Articles

  • A Primer on Distressed Investing – Moyer, Martin & Martin (JACF, 2012)
  • Hedge Funds and Chapter 11 – Jiang, Li & Wang (Journal of Finance, 2012)
  • Rent Extraction by Super-Priority Lenders – Eckbo, Li & Wang (SSRN/Tuck, 2019–2020)
  • The Hertz Maneuver – Casey & Macey (U. Chicago L. Rev. Online, 2020)
  • The Expected Costs of Distress – Glover (JFE, 2016)
  • The Ownership and Trading of Debt Claims in Chapter 11 – Ivashina, Iverson & Smith (JFE, 2016)
